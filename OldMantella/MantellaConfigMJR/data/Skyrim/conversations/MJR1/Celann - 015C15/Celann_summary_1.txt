Celann and Agmaer converse late at night in a dimly lit, disheveled stone chamber of the fort, complaining about the 1 AM hour while polishing axes amid howling wolves outside. They speculate on vampires stirring in the canyon, noting the room's chaos—overturned crates, a half-made bed, dusty alcoves once used for holy water from old Vigil days, crooked banners, a flickering lantern, and an ajar door suggesting ambush or hasty flight. Agmaer shares his nerves from a past wolf encounter and requests mead to ease his dry throat, while Celann dismisses fears, boasting about Isran's stakes for vampire kebabs and the fort's defenses against the cold wind and potential threats from under the long table or barrels of rotten mead.
