In a dimly lit back hall of an inn in Windpeak at dawn, illuminated by flickering torchlight and greenish auroras filtering through narrow windows, Karita complains about the biting cold and the fire's meager warmth, while noting the group's weary appearance and inquiring about their early rise. Lydia, the housecarl to her Thane, embraces the frost as a familiar Nord hardship and praises the incense's comforting scent, revealing they're up due to threats from lurking bandits who have been harassing Dawnstar's mines and endangering honest folk amid a town plagued by widespread nightmares of claws and shadows. Karita adds that her father is increasingly agitated by these disturbances, mistaking the inn's tense, ritual-like atmosphere—with hooded figures, cluttered tables of vials and scrolls, and hanging tapestries—for a temple, though she clarifies it's just the early hour's eerie glow.
