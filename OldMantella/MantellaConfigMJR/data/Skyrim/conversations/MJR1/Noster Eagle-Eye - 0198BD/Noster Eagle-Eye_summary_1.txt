In the dimly lit cobblestone alleyway of Solitude under a starry night sky, Serana banters with the one-eyed beggar Noster Eagle-Eye about the poetic injustice of the wealthy in the Blue Palace ignoring street rats, invoking Dibella's free love ideals while Noster shares his Great War scars from Anvil and laments the quiet nights reminiscent of Thalmor ambushes, skeptically dismissing divine aid and pleading for a septim amid the flickering lanterns and shadowed paths; Serana sympathizes with his Imperial hardships, mocks Skyrim's false peace under Thalmor influence, and probes if he's ever scouted a vampire nest.
