[Day 136, 6 in the early morning]
In the rain-swept ruins of Vominheim, with Serana, Lydia, Barney, Jo'rra, and Goat nearby, Soraul Anvil-Finger remarked to Frea on the cursed atmosphere under the stormy sky, likening it to bruised flesh and stirring Nord spirits, prompting Frea to note the storm as Dibella's tears mirroring Solstheim's unrest and share her experience hunting beasts in worse blizzards while asking what drew him there so early. Soraul then poetically compared the surrounding birch trees to silent sentinels reminiscent of Windhelm's forges and inquired about the spirits' whispers in such nights, to which Frea replied that the snowfall veiled ancient secrets like forgotten prayers, evoking her village rites of dancing bare under auroras and channeling rage into strikes, before complimenting his Nord build and asking if he'd ever forged weapons with his own sweat and fire. Soraul acknowledged the spirits' murmurs of blood-forged glories but shifted focus, declaring her hammer's call clear when metal sings true, at which point the player interjected, telling Frea to forget the "idiot" and urging them to get going.
