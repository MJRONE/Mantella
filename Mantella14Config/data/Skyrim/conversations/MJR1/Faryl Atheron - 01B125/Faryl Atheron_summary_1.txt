Under a vibrant aurora borealis illuminating a snowy clearing near a weathered wooden lodge in Windhelm's Gray Quarter, Serana admires the ethereal green and purple sky, quipping about the gods' crude artistry and Dibella's ironic blessings amid the cold. Faryl Atheron, a local Dunmer laborer, banters back skeptically about beauty in their harsh surroundings, complaining of his grueling farm work under overseer Bolfrida and the salted soil, while Serana retorts with crude humor comparing it to a divine mishap and dismisses the Gray Quarter as a Dunmer slum; Faryl notes her unusually filthy tongue compared to typical elven complaints.
