Under a vast, starlit night sky near a remote wooden shack in Whiterun's wilderness, Serana teasingly remarks on the clear view for spotting Stormcloak spies or indulging in Dibella-inspired fantasies with bandits, while the Whiterun Guard dismisses her vampiric musings about peeping stars from Oblivion and grumbles about freezing on patrol. He identifies the nearby building as a hunter's shack, threatening to chain her there at dawn if she persists, prompting her retort about his tight oaths to Jarl Balgruuf after too much mead. As midnight deepens, the Guard expresses hope that the lantern-lit structure is an inn with hot stew rather than a Daedric trap, to which Serana flirts by calling herself a centuries-old trap and invites him closer under the stars, suggesting its rooms are ideal for sharing Imperial secrets.
