In the warmly lit early morning ambiance of The Winking Skeever in Solitude, with its crackling hearth casting flickering light over scarred wooden tables, steaming mugs of mead, and nearby patrons including Serana, Lydia, Frea, Corpulus Vinius behind the bar, and Belrand nursing a drink, Gulum-Ei, seated in his shadowy alcove with forest-green scales glinting faintly, addresses Noster Eagle-Eye with a gruff hmph, commenting on the tavern's chill paling against the docks' frost, teasing the old beggar for nursing his mug all night, and noting how the fire barely wards off the ghosts, while an ethereal observation unfolds of a distant, dimly lit stone chamber deep within a mountain hold—its weathered Nordic rune-etched walls, vault door, braziers, dusty tomes, potion vials, alchemical workbench, and alcoves stocked with maps and artifacts evoking a hushed, forgotten sanctum—without direct response from Noster or the player amid the tavern's hushed murmur.
